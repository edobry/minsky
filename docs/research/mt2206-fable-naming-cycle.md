# mt#2206 — Fable naming cycle: blind Lexicon pass, screen, and Opus comparison

**Date:** 2026-07-08 · **Status:** research artifact (provenance) · **Feeds:** mt#2046 umbrella (keep/rename/defer verdict) · **Method:** single-agent blind Lexicon pass on Claude Fable 5, compared post-hoc against the June 2026 Opus 4.8 four-pass artifact (`mt2206-lexicon-naming-shortlist.md`)

The explicit purpose of this cycle: test whether a different model searches the name-space differently. Protocol: **generate first, compare second** — the Opus artifact was not read until Fable's own candidates were generated and screened.

**Blindness caveat (recorded for integrity):** the tasking prompt itself disclosed the Opus finalist _names_ (Nervecord, Orrery, Cordmeld, Pleromesh, Nerveclad; Daimon/Quipu flagged; Exo as prior frontrunner). Blindness is therefore partial at the name level and full at the reasoning/generation level. To preserve independence, Fable **deliberately generated nothing in the `nerve-` / `cord-` / `exo-` prefix families and avoided the daimon/quipu fields** — so absence of those veins in the Fable set is a blinding artifact, not a search miss. Everything else was generated cold.

---

## Headline

- **The saturation finding replicates on a second model.** 11 of 16 Fable-screened candidates died (69% kill rate) — the trademark/domain screen, not generation quality, is the binding constraint, exactly as the Opus pass found (91% kill in its v2).
- **Same saturated core, two opposite escape routes.** Opus escaped _upward_ — coined compounds from orthogonal conceptual pillars (Eva-umbilical, gnostic). Fable escaped _downward_ — obscure **real** operational words from under-trafficked physical domains (nautical maneuvers, heraldry, principal's-agent social roles). Both routes produce clean candidates; they trade fluency against tangibility differently.
- **One genuine cross-model convergence:** both searches independently landed on the "instrument that renders coordinated motion legible" territory (Opus: **Orrery**; Fable: **Zoetrope**, plus Capstan/Pantograph in the same register). Independent replication is evidence that this conceptual territory is load-bearing for the myth.
- **Fable adds two screened survivors Opus never generated:** **Kedge** and **Vexil**, plus two clean second-tier names (Binnacle, Retinue).

## Comparison with the Opus pass (read only after the above existed)

### Method contrast

|                  | Opus (June 2026)                                                                                                  | Fable (this pass)                                                              |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |

### Overlap

- **Both passes killed the same saturated middle:** Cortex/Axon-family, Ganglia (Opus: domains gone; Fable: connotation + Ganglia monitoring), the descriptive-operational tier.
- **Convergence on the coordinated-motion instrument:** Opus's **Orrery** and Fable's **Zoetrope** are the same semiotic move — a pre-electronic instrument that makes many independent motions legible as one system. Fable's Capstan and Pantograph are adjacent (coordinated force / motion-at-scale) and died only on screens. This territory is independently replicated → high confidence it fits the myth.
- **The saturation thesis itself** — trademark screen as binding constraint — replicated at a lower kill rate (69% vs 91%), likely _because_ Fable's vocabulary was more obscure on average.

### Divergence — regions each model reached that the other didn't

**Fable surfaced, Opus absent:**

1. **Nautical maneuver vocabulary** (Kedge, Capstan, Binnacle) — Opus's physical-domain briefs (v1/v2) produced studio/control-room instruments (Patchbay, Flightdeck, Setpoint) but no seamanship. Kedge is the standout: a real single-syllable verb-able word whose denotation _is_ the orchestration loop.
2. **Principal's-agent social roles** (Reeve, Retinue, Factor, Adjutant) — the most _literal_ myth denotations in either pass (a retinue is exactly "intent-bearing servitors attending a principal"); entirely missing from the Opus pillar basis. Mostly screen-killed or statement-mode, but the field itself was unmined.
3. **Heraldry/vexillology** (Vexil) — the only candidate in either pool carrying both a V and an X.

**Opus surfaced, Fable absent (with cause):**

1. **Eva-umbilical / neural-tether coinages** (Nervecord, Cordmeld, Nerveclad) — blinding artifact: Fable deliberately avoided `nerve-`/`cord-` after prompt disclosure. Fable's _independent_ neural vein (Vagus, Sinew, Plexus) was all real-word anatomy, and all of it screen-died — weak evidence that in the neural field, only coinage escapes the screens, which corroborates the Opus v3 finding.
2. **Gnostic/egregore** (Pleromesh; Daimon's guiding-spirit read) — a deliberate register call, not a miss (see Phase 2 note). Fable's read of `minsky-brand` §7 excludes occult-coded heads; the Opus pass treated the field as high-quota. Principal adjudication needed if these candidates advance.
3. **Externalized-memory devices** (Quipu) — genuinely not reached by Fable's framings; the synchronicity rotation had no "record-keeping instrument" leg. A real search miss.

### What the model swap actually bought

The two passes are **complementary, not redundant**: Opus's escape (coinage from conceptual pillars) buys cleanliness at the cost of processing fluency (Pleromesh F=3, Cordmeld coined); Fable's escape (obscure real words from unfashionable physical domains) buys tangibility and real-word fluency-on-sight at the cost of lexical familiarity (Kedge F≈3) — and demonstrates that **real-word + clean is achievable**, which the Opus artifact's v2 had concluded was nearly closed (3/33, all mid-fit). The union of both fronts is stronger than either alone.
